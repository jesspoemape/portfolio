Backstory

I grew up in Texas, and I came to Utah when I was 18 to attend Brigham Young University. Ever since I was small, I wanted to be an engineer. I was always interested in fixing things and making things. I went to a high school that specialized in science, technology, and engineering.

When I started at BYU, I was studying Civil Engineering. I did this for two years, so I took several math, science and engineering classes. After two years, I decided to switch my major to Spanish. I definitely think it was the right decision for me to switch majors to Spanish because I enjoy the humanities.

After I graduated, I was contemplating my options for careers and continued education. I knew I had the skills to do more technical things like math and coding because of my background in engineering. I decided I wanted to attend DevMountain to become a web developer and fulfill my dream of being an engineer.
